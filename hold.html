entry / emotional archive hold

hold

/hoʊld/ verb noun

to keep something from falling

to remain in contact

to resist release

to preserve through touch or pressure

Some things are not kept by memory alone.

They are held in the body — in the hand that does not move, in the shoulder that carries weight, in the pause before letting go.

To hold is not always gentle. Sometimes it means keeping something from disappearing by applying just enough pressure.
